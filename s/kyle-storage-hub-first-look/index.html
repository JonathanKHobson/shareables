What Kyle Storage Hub is

Kyle Storage Hub is best understood as a control surface, not as the final home of every piece of source content. The vault organizes project maps, MCP records, area notes, and file cards while the underlying project material lives across attached drives and working directories.

That distinction matters. The value of the vault is not simply that it stores notes; it makes a large, multi-location body of work navigable. It gives Jonathan a way to see what exists, what is active, and what is close to becoming a public artifact.

The result is a system that is more useful than a personal archive and more legible than a loose folder tree. For teaching, consulting, and public credibility, that means the work can be explained, audited, and turned into shareable outputs without starting from zero each time.

What's active now

MCP infrastructure

Shareables MCP, Kyle Second Brain, Brain and Local Storage, ChatGPT History MCP, Discord Digest MCP, and Kyle Manager Core form the operating layer. These are the systems Jonathan uses to run, retrieve, and ship other work.

Reasoning and knowledge systems

A cluster of reasoning, glossary, and structured thinking projects forms one of the clearest public-output lanes in the vault. It is the part of the system most ready to become explainers, guides, and teaching artifacts.

Games and campaigns

Peasants to Kings, Peril to Profit, and Stargate SG-1 represent the campaign side of the ecosystem. These are supported by game-master tooling, memory, and stateful project structure rather than isolated session notes.
