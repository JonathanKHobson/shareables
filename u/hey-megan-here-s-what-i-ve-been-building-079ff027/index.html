A little context first

So you know how I've always talked about wanting to do something *meaningful* with AI — not just prompt ChatGPT but actually build infrastructure? This is that.

Over the past several months I've been constructing what I think of as a personal AI ecosystem — tools that think alongside me, not just answer me. Each of these three projects is public, live on the web, and built entirely by me using a combination of code, design, and a lot of obsessive iteration.

They're also things I think a curious, thoughtful person like you would find genuinely interesting — not just as "Kyle's hobby" but as ideas worth poking at.

---

The three things I built

### Thing 1: An epistemic audit tool

What it is: You paste a piece of writing, a claim, or a document, and it stress-tests it. What assumptions are hiding inside? What biases are baked in? Is the evidence actually sound, or does it just *sound* sound?

Why I built it: I was frustrated with AI tools that just agreed with everything. This one is built to push back. The knowledge base I constructed for it has 1,295+ entries covering logical fallacies, cognitive biases, non-Western philosophical frameworks (Buddhist logic, Indigenous epistemologies, African traditions), and structured reasoning models.

Go explore it: jonathankhobson.github.io/critical-compass

---

### Thing 2: A prompt engineering guide

What it is: Prompt engineering is the skill of writing instructions to an AI model in a way that actually gets you what you want. I turned everything I know about it into a searchable, structured reference — a style guide for talking to AI.

Why I built it: Most people talk to AI like they'd talk to a search engine — short, keyword-heavy, impatient. That gets mediocre results. This is a resource for doing it better, organized around patterns, techniques, and worked examples.

Go explore it: jonathankhobson.github.io/prompt-compass

### Thing 3: What is an MCP?

What it is: A plain-language explainer for a genuinely new thing in AI. MCP stands for Model Context Protocol — the emerging standard for connecting AI models to real tools (calendars, databases, apps, file systems). Think of it like USB for AI: one standard that lets any AI plug into any service.

Why I built it: Every explanation I found was either too technical or too vague. Mine starts with "here's what it does for you" and builds from there.

Go explore it: jonathankhobson.github.io/what-is-an-mcp

What is an MCP, really?

Let me give you the version that doesn't require a computer science degree.

Imagine you have a really smart assistant — but right now, they can only talk to you through a window. They can answer questions, but they can't actually *do* anything in your world: they can't check your calendar, search your files, send a message, or look something up in real time.

MCP is the protocol — think of it as a shared agreement about how to communicate — that lets that assistant reach through the window and actually use tools. It's standardized, so any AI model can plug into any compatible tool without anyone needing to write custom plumbing every time.

Right now, most AI is stuck giving you words. MCP is the infrastructure that lets AI become genuinely *agentic* — able to take actions, retrieve real information, and work inside your actual systems. It's a small technical thing with enormous implications.

---

Why I built all of this

Honestly? Frustration. Most AI tools are built for the median use case — quick answers, simple summaries, content generation on demand. I wanted tools that could handle harder, messier cognitive work: pressure-testing ideas, structured reasoning, technical infrastructure.

I'm also deeply convinced that AI is going to touch every knowledge worker's life in the next few years. The people who understand *how* it works — not just that it works — are going to have a significant advantage. These tools are partly my way of working that out in public.

And if they're useful to you, or someone you know, that genuinely makes my day. Drop me a note and tell me what you think.
